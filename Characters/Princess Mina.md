## Basic information

- **Age:** 21
- **Title:** Third Princess of Twiceland / Youngest daughter of the Royal Family
- **Nicknames:** "The Silent Princess," "The Black Swan"
- **Prophetic name (Verenthaal):** Neyrene (Ch. 9) / Tikhaya — "The Silent Princess" is a translation of her prophetic name. The nickname was never innocent.

## Core contrast with Chaeyoung

|Chaeyoung|Mina|
|---|---|
|Acts without thinking|Thinks without acting|
|Doesn't value her own life|Doesn't feel her life is real|
|Hides pain behind strength|Hides desires behind composure|
|Needs someone to make her stay|Needs someone to make her move|
|Shows love through protection|Shows love through attention|
|Afraid of arriving too late|Afraid of never starting|

**What Chaeyoung gives Mina:** Reality. The world outside books. Permission to want things. Being seen as a person, not a title.

**What Mina gives Chaeyoung:** Worth. The radical idea that her life matters. A reason to come back alive. Being known, not just respected.

## Personality

**On the surface:** Elegant, composed, soft-spoken, graceful. The perfect princess. She knows exactly how to sit, smile, make a guest feel welcome. She reads, drinks tea, writes poetry.

**Underneath:** Restless. Deeply, silently restless. She reads obsessively not because she loves peace, but because books are the only way to experience a world she has never been allowed to touch. Every novel is a life she'll never live. Every map is a place she'll never visit.

**Observational intelligence:** She notices everything. The way servants look down when they lie, how Nayeon's smile tightens when she's worried, how Chaeyoung favors her left side after a fight. She collects details about people the way others collect coins. In Ch. 5, she counts the intervals of Chaeyoung's weight shifts (3 seconds less on the left side = shoulder bothering her). She recognizes that counting isn't normal.

**Core flaw:** Passivity. Not for lack of courage — she has been trained her entire life to believe her role is to stay still. She asks permission when she should demand. She hints when she should speak directly. The book theft (Ch. 5) is her first active break.

**What makes her angry:** Being ignored. Being talked about as if she weren't there. The prophecy — not because it frightens her, but because it reduces her to a thing.

**What makes her happy:** Learning something new. A perfect verse. The moment someone drops their formal mask and speaks to her like a real person. Being useful beyond her title.

## Life in the castle — The gilded cage

She has never left the castle grounds until Ch. 7. Not because there's a rule — no one thought she needed a reason to.

Structured days: lessons in the morning, tea in the afternoon, reading at night. She attends events when required. Smiles when expected.

She has her favorite terrace (where Chaeyoung meets her in Ch. 1), her corner in the library, her routines. Everything she could want — except freedom.

"The Black Swan" — a nickname born of whispers about the prophecy, a reference to the anomaly. Mina believes she's called that for her silent elegance. When she discovers the truth, even her nickname will feel like another lie.

## Relationships

**Nayeon:** Complex. Mina loves Nayeon but there is a wall of duty and protection. Nayeon treats her as something fragile. Mina resents this silently. The Ch. 6 confrontation (about the prophecy/powers) is the breaking point: Mina realizes Nayeon sees her as someone to protect, not respect as an equal. In Ch. 7, Nayeon authorizes the trip to Bryndell — "one less latch on a door that had been closed for twenty-one years." She processes guilt over the years of the gilded cage.

**Sana:** Warmer. Sana is the bridge between Nayeon's seriousness and Mina's quietness. She's the only one who can make Mina laugh without trying. But even with Sana, Mina holds back. Sana is sunshine — Mina doesn't want to cloud her.

**Chaeyoung:** → See progression section below and `Couples.md`.

**Miyoung (Ch. 7):** Immediate connection. Mina speaks sign language fluently — she learned an entire language on the chance she'd one day meet Chaeyoung's mother. Miyoung recognizes in Mina the same expression Hajoon had. She asks Mina to take care of Chaeyoung. Silent adoption.

## The secret reader

She has been reading about magic long before the story begins. It started innocently: a book on sorcery in a forgotten corner. Then she noticed the restricted section — books in chains, shelves behind locked doors. She tells herself it's academic curiosity. It's something deeper: a pull, as if the books were calling to her.

**The blood of the lost lineage of sorceresses is awakening. The magic was always there, dormant.**

The books she steals with Chaeyoung in Ch. 5 are not random. She knows exactly which ones she wants. She planned it for weeks. Chaeyoung thinks it's spontaneous. It isn't.

**Previous books (hidden):** _Principles of Elemental Energy_, _Theory of Flow and Form_, handwritten notebook with cramped/urgent handwriting (mentions five texts from the restricted section).

**Recovered books (Ch. 5):** _On the Nature of the Unborn Power_, thick volume with blue-edged pages, _Documented Cases of Late Manifestation in Dormant Lineages_ (unexpected find).

**Current location:** Drawer under clothing in the royal wing antechamber (not the false panel — that's in her original chambers).

## Powers

**Nature:** Pure energy — creation and destruction in their most basic forms. Not elemental. She controls the essence of things itself. This is what makes the prophecy so threatening.

**Early manifestations (pre-Ch. 6):**

- Warmth in the palms (months, no apparent pattern)
- Cups that move, pages that turn on their own
- Breezes in closed rooms
- Candles that flicker when she's emotional

**Awakening (Ch. 6):**

- Increased gravity → first woman pinned to the floor → sword falls
- Second woman: door held shut by invisible force
- Two women lifted centimeter by centimeter — "like something being raised"
- Cracking ribs: "bones curving beyond their tolerance. Not broken. Not yet."
- Mina split into two perceptions: one registers the room, the other is "somewhere else — warm, deep, without edges"
- The candle: burns without flame — white, pure light
- Chaeyoung enters against the pressure (six steps). They take each other's hands → two frequencies finding each other

**Post-awakening (Ch. 7):**

- Latent mode. Warm palms, subtle manifestations.
- Outside the castle: grass sways without wind. Power responds to openness/space.
- No disruptive manifestations.

**Progression:** Magic responds more to emotion than to will. Calm = small, precise things. Frightened or angry = things break.

**Fear:** Terrified of herself. Not of the power, but of what it means. If the prophecy is real, she is a weapon. And weapons don't choose.

**Connection with Chaeyoung:** Mina's magic calms near Chaeyoung. When she's close, candles don't flicker, the warmth settles. The Night Veil blood doesn't just protect physically — it anchors her magic.

## Secrets

**1. The books:** She has been studying magic far longer than anyone knows. The theft was calculated. Hidden collection.

**2. The sensation:** She noticed the magic months ago. Small things she dismissed. She has been frightened and silent.

**3. The dreams:** She dreams of a place she has never been. A tower by the sea, covered in vines, with books that write themselves. She doesn't know it yet: she is seeing Aethermoor — the city of her mother's lineage.

**4. What she feels for Chaeyoung:** She knows. Unlike Chaeyoung, she recognizes the feeling early. She knows it's inappropriate, impossible, potentially dangerous. She feels it anyway. Ch. 5: she identifies her state as an "incomplete hypothesis" — gives herself a rational explanation ("gratitude") and recognizes it as too quick = evidence against herself.

**5. Her mother's secret:** She doesn't know she has a different father than Nayeon and Sana. She has noticed she looks different, that she is drawn to things (magic, ancient languages, melodies) that no one else in the family is. The Queen watches her with an expression she can't decipher — not just maternal love, something heavier. The revelation will destroy and liberate her simultaneously.

## Progression through chapters

**Ch. 1–2:** The perfect portrait. Still, beautiful, silent. She exists inside the frame. First encounter with Chaeyoung on the terrace — formal curiosity.

**Ch. 3–4:** Silences that change shape. "I wish you wouldn't. Get hurt, I mean." The distance shortens.

**Ch. 5:** Complicity. She planned the book theft. Chooses bare truth: "These are texts about ancient energies. About power." Asks specifically for Chaeyoung's help ("I'm asking you to break rules with me"). Transforms in the restricted section ("Lit up"). Counts weight-shift intervals. Registers the absence of contact with "absurd clarity." Trembling hands after the column → "it's adrenaline." Leaves the door ajar.

**Ch. 6:** Rupture. First uncontrolled awakening. "Mina" without a title for the first time. "You owe me a story" — raw, direct, no layers. Confrontation with Nayeon about the prophecy. Letter to the great-grandmother.

**Ch. 7:** Maximum evolution in a single chapter. From passive to active: asks to go outside, asks to go to Bryndell, speaks sign language fluently, translates for Chaeyoung, offers to teach. First time outside the walls ("It's bigger than I imagined"). Bow to Miyoung: person to person, not protocol. Red to her ears when Miyoung asks if she has someone waiting for her. "Not today. Today my name is Mina" — reclaims the name. Takes Chaeyoung's hands to correct finger position. "When you say it to your mother, make sure it's true." "You should do that more often."

## Overall arc

**Beginning — The perfect portrait:** She exists inside the frame others built.

**Development — The frame cracks:** Chaeyoung arrives and is everything the castle isn't. For the first time, Mina wants something she can't find in a book.

**Conflict — The prophecy and the power:** The combined truth of the powers awakening pulls her out of passivity. Everyone has an opinion about what she should be. Mina wants to choose for herself.

**Crisis — The weight of power:** A moment where her power is necessary. Using it = risking becoming the weapon. Holding back = letting people she loves get hurt. (Connects with Chaeyoung: if Mina doesn't act, Chaeyoung will sacrifice herself.)

**Resolution — The author of her own story:** Power isn't inherently destructive. Being strong isn't being a weapon. She chooses to use her power on her own terms. Mina, who spent her life reading others' words, finally writes her own.
