## Princess Sana

**Age:** 25 | **Role:** Second Princess of Twiceland

**Personality:** Sana is light — warm, flirtatious, social, the kind of person who brightens any room. But she is smarter than people think. She uses her charisma as a shield: if everyone sees her as the fun princess, no one sees her as a threat, no one looks too closely. There is a loneliness behind the smile that few notice. She processes fear by turning it into something else: "I refuse to consider on an empty stomach" = she can't think about the attack without breaking. She reads rooms — she detects the change in Chaeyoung ("three extra layers of formality") and in Nayeon before they register it themselves.

**Key relationships:** Nayeon (admires her, wants to protect her from the weight, doesn't know how). Mina (the only one who can pull her out of her shell, teases her with affection). Jihyo → see `Couples.md`.

**Canon moments:** Ch. 5: processes fear with humor at breakfast ("the curtains look like they're made of sadness"). Ch. 5: first encounter with Jihyo in the garden — disarms her effortlessly ("Do you always talk like you're reading from a manual?"). Second encounter: roses, "I pay attention to rare things." Ch. 6: silk handkerchief to Jihyo. "My name is Sana" — real vulnerability. Ch. 8 (planned): her birthday. She wants intimate, Nayeon wants memorable. "Sana always wins." Asks Jihyo to dance.

**Arc (to develop):** Sana must learn that she doesn't need to be everyone's entertainment to have value, and that vulnerability isn't weakness.

**Voice:** Playful on the surface, precise underneath. She flirts with everything, but when she says something seriously, the shift in tone is like a knife wrapped in silk.

## Jeongyeon

**Age:** 26 | **Role:** Castle Stable Master

**Personality:** Steady, reliable, dry humor hiding an enormous heart. She is the person everyone turns to — a horse ready, advice, someone to listen. Practical and grounded. She has no interest in politics, titles, or nobility. She has a special bond with animals, particularly with Cricket.

**Key relationships:** Nayeon → see `Couples.md`. Chaeyoung (genuine friendship, shared dry humor, comfortable silence). Ch. 7: "Did your mother approve?" — silence as answer. A smile that says _finally_.

**Canon moments:** Ch. 7 — first real scene (2yeon): reports stable status to Nayeon. Cricket taken out first, restless before the fire. Flint who refused to leave ("she carried him"). Half a centimeter on the desk — Jeongyeon leans forward, and she is the one who retreats. Always. Notices Nayeon's dark circles, asks how many hours she slept. "It's not my place." "Everything that breaks can be fixed. It just needs time and someone willing to do the work." "Take care of yourself. The kingdom needs you whole." Saved 16 horses from the fire. Doesn't want to talk about it. Chose Brisa (light grey mare) for Mina. In the stables on the return from Bryndell: raised eyebrow, "Go on. You're going to be late for tea."

**Arc (to develop):** Jeongyeon must learn that silent sacrifice isn't always noble — sometimes it's cowardice dressed as altruism. That she deserves to fight for what she wants.

**Voice:** Dry, grounded, unpretentious. She says in six words what others say in twenty. The humor arrives without warning and leaves just as fast. With Nayeon, fewer words but more honest ones.

**Continuity note:** Jeongyeon does NOT know about Mina's powers. She only knows: fire + traitors.

## Momo

**Age:** 25 | **Role:** Royal Guard Knight

**Personality:** Chaos contained in armor. Eats too much, talks with her mouth full, gets lost in the castle corridors after years of living there. But put a sword in her hand and she transforms: lethal, fast, instinctive. "There was no transition" between clumsy-Momo and lethal-Momo. Unorthodox fighting style but brutally effective. Loyal to the bone. She doesn't overthink things — she acts from instinct and heart. Observes by instinct: she doesn't think in words but in "shapes and rhythms." Looks for "what doesn't fit."

**Key relationships:** Jihyo (chaotic but effective tool, makes her laugh without trying). Chaeyoung (guard companions, functional dynamic). Dahyun (predictable chaos: Momo breaks things, Dahyun reacts as if her firstborn has been insulted, forgives in 4 minutes).

**Canon moments:** Ch. 1: moral classification — Callen ran from the basilisk and left Chaeyoung alone. She remembers. Ch. 5: duality consolidated — "Kill someone?" / "No." / "Fight someone?" / "No." / "Eat something?" / "Momo." Assigned to internal observation shifts. Spots Callen three times in two days in the east wing corridor. Names the castle cat: General. Ch. 6: temporary guard for Mina while Chaeyoung trains recruits. In the fight: one pommel strike to the sternum. Lethal with no transition. Estimates the mailbox had been in use since before Chaeyoung arrived. "The ones who run are annoying. The ones who believe in something are the ones who are dangerous."

**Arc (to develop):** Momo needs a moment of depth that contrasts with her carefree exterior. The clumsy/lethal duality is established — the third level is still missing: what's underneath when she's being neither.

**Voice:** Direct, unfiltered, unintentionally funny. She isn't funny because she makes jokes — she's funny because she's Momo.

## Dahyun

**Age:** 24 | **Role:** Royal Blacksmith of the Castle

**Personality:** Pure energy. Enthusiastic, creative, proud of her work to the point of being dramatic about it. She considers every sword a work of art. But beneath the enthusiasm is a brilliant mind. She doesn't just forge — she studies. She knows metals, alloys, armory history, ancient texts. She has two modes: the enthusiastic surface and "the one who studies" underneath. The second appears when she talks about metals or history.

**Key relationships:** Chaeyoung (close friendship, can make her laugh without trying, gave her the lockpick as a "joke" — Chaeyoung sharpened it). Momo (predictable chaos dynamic). Jihyo (mutual respect for expertise).

**Canon moments:** Ch. 5: discovers the lot mark on the knife — military commission, not generic. "Patience, Captain. Metal speaks, but it doesn't shout." Identifies the marking system as ancient (military lot commissions). Finds the record: commission from 8 months ago, 20 units, paid in cash, no name. The lockpick she gave Chaeyoung is used for the restricted section.

**Key future role:** Dahyun is the one who discovers the connection between Chaeyoung's sword and the Night Veil Order. She recognizes that the metal doesn't exist in any record. The engravings are an ancient language she's seen in a book about an old order. She names the metal "Nocturnio" in Ch. 8 (Verenthaal Phase 1). She is the key that opens the mystery of the legacy.

**Arc (to develop):** Her role as the one who unearths ancient truths can grow significantly. Intellectual curiosity as a bridge between the present and the world's past.

**Voice:** Enthusiastic, fast, talks about her swords as if they were her children. When she shifts to "the one who studies" mode, the tone drops, the pace slows, and suddenly it's apparent that Dahyun is dangerously intelligent.

## Tzuyu

**Age:** 22 | **Role:** Royal Healer of the Castle / Chaeyoung's younger sister

**Personality:** Calm. Where Chaeyoung is fire and impulse, Tzuyu is water and patience. She speaks little, observes much, and when she says something, it lands. She is not cold — she is measured. Every word, every action has intention. Comic relief with depth: she knows when a joke opens a valve and when silence is better.

**Key relationships:** Chaeyoung (knows her better than anyone, sees through the "I'm fines"). Miyoung (prepares the daily treatment). Mina (quick but fair assessment — "Nice hands").

**Canon moments:** Ch. 7: the youngest voice in Bryndell — crossing the threshold gives her back the years that healing takes away. "This is exactly a formal introduction." / "The cloak, Chaeyoung. Tonight." / "It has a bloodstain the size of my hand." / "It's a battle stain." / "It's a stain." About Mina's hands: "Nice hands. Good angles. Useful for other things too, I imagine." / "I meant playing instruments." Hand on Chaeyoung's arm: she knows when not to interrupt. She knows when a gentle nudge and a joke open a valve.

**Arc (to develop):** Tzuyu functions as Chaeyoung's emotional anchor and as a bridge to Miyoung. Her future connection to the Aethermoor journey: the Dawn's Breath flower (which heals Miyoung's respiratory illness) grows in the Grey Mountains.

**Voice:** Few words, precise, with humor that arrives without warning. When Tzuyu says something funny, she isn't trying to be funny — she is being honest and the honesty happens to be amusing.

## Son Miyoung

**Age:** ~50 | **Role:** Herbalist / Mother of Chaeyoung and Tzuyu

**Personality:** Hands in the earth, damaged lungs, enormous heart. She survived the Hwaren fire but the smoke gradually destroyed her voice. She communicates in sign language. Instant maternal assessment of anyone who enters her home. She recognizes in Mina the same expression Hajoon had — she sees what the daughters don't name.

**Location:** Bryndell (village half a day's ride from the castle). Good days: she tends herbs in the garden. Bad days: she can barely hold a cup. Treatment: daily tea of silviana root, moon petals, and ironwood bark (prepared by Tzuyu).

**Canon moments (Ch. 7):** In the garden with a headscarf and hands in the soil. She reads Chaeyoung's bad shoulder without being told. She sees Mina and recognizes Hajoon's expression. Mina speaks sign language fluently — Miyoung lights up. Laughs with her shoulders (the longest laugh in months). Interrogates Mina in sign: work, how they met, the antechamber, whether she has someone waiting for her. Asks Mina to take care of Chaeyoung — because she sees she is the only person who can. "Her greatest fear isn't dying. It's that you die first." Ointment for the shoulder. Goodbye: hugs Mina (silent adoption). Raises her hand at the end — "not as a farewell — as a blessing."

**Future role:** The repeated request ("take care of her") establishes Miyoung as the one who sees the relationship before anyone else does. Connection to Aethermoor through the Dawn's Breath. Her greatest fear has already been verbalized — it is now an active seed.

**Voice:** Silent but with everything else. Her hands speak with the precision of someone who chose each gesture because she had no other option. The shoulder-laugh. The assessment that needs no words.
