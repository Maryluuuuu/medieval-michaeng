She didn't see the blow coming.

The troll's mace struck her full in the side, ripping the air from her lungs and sending her flying like a rag doll. The world spun around her: sky, ground, sky, ground.

Chaeyoung hit the ground hard, rolling several meters through the mud and fallen bodies. Her helmet flew off at some point during the impact, and her shield split in two against a rock.

For a moment, everything was silent. A sharp ringing in her ears. The metallic taste of blood in her mouth.

_Get up._

Her body wasn't responding. The pain in her side was searing, paralyzing.

_Get up, damn it!_

Through her blurred vision, she saw the troll approaching. Each step made the ground tremble. It dragged its mace, leaving a furrow in the dirt, taking its time. Savoring the victory.

Chaeyoung gritted her teeth and forced her hand to search for her sword. It was still there, miraculously. Her fingers closed around the handle.

The troll raised the mace above its head, grunting with satisfaction.

Chaeyoung waited.

One second. The mace began to descend.

She rolled.

The weapon crashed into the ground where her head had been, sending up a shower of dirt and stones. Chaeyoung was already on her feet, staggering but standing, her sword at the ready.

The troll roared, furious at having missed.

"My turn," Chaeyoung murmured, spitting blood.

She launched herself forward. She dodged a clumsy swipe, sliding under the creature's arm, and drove her sword into its armpit, where the skin was softer. The troll shrieked. Chaeyoung twisted the blade, ripping it out with a brutal movement.

Before the monster could react, she slid between its legs, her sword cutting the tendon of its ankle in one clean motion. The creature howled in pain, staggering, and Chaeyoung took advantage to climb up its back before it could recover.

A single cut to the nape. Deep. Precise.

The troll collapsed like a felled tree, shaking the ground as it fell.

Chaeyoung jumped from its back before it hit the ground, landing on her feet, barely. Her side screamed with pain at every breath. She probably had something broken. Or fractured. Or both.

But she was alive.

She allowed herself a second to catch her breath, leaning on her sword, and looked up at the sky.

The dawn was beautiful. A soft gradient of blues and oranges spread across the horizon. It would have been a lovely place for a morning stroll, if not for the clamor of clashing metal and furious gallops around her.

Chaeyoung gripped her sword firmly, both hands squeezing the handle. She was breathing rapidly, trying to steady it, inhaling deeply on occasion.

Sweat dripped down her forehead, mixing along the way with her blood and the dirt of the battlefield.

"Advance!" Jihyo's firm voice resonated over the chaos, her sword pointing forward.

The trolls were beginning to retreat, some running in terror, others dragging themselves due to their wounds.

Chaeyoung quickly approached her captain, scanning the surroundings. "They'll try to regroup in the forest," she warned between ragged breaths. "Have the archers finish off the rest."

Jihyo nodded immediately, trusting the judgment of her second-in-command. "Don't let them escape!" She turned toward the field to give the signal to the archers.

"Take aim!" Chaeyoung shouted toward the line of archers, coordinating the attack. "Fire!"

A rain of arrows covered the beautiful dawn sky, followed by the whistle of projectiles falling a few meters from them. Trolls falling shortly after. And suddenly, peace after the storm. Occasionally, a groan of pain here and there, both human and troll.

Jihyo scanned the area, searching for any surviving monster. Chaeyoung approached her, also breathing heavily.

"Everything seems in order, Captain," Chaeyoung commented.

"Send the scouts to the forest," Jihyo ordered with authority. "I don't want any of those things escaping."

Chaeyoung quickly complied, transmitting the order to the nearby soldiers.

---

At first, Jihyo hadn't taken the news well when they told her someone younger would be her second-in-command. "The best of her year," they told her.

The first months were difficult for both. The older woman was always looking to challenge Chaeyoung. They fought on several occasions while training, some blows harder than they should be for a training session.

It wasn't until a few months ago that Jihyo valued the true capabilities of her companion. On one of their expeditions, on a mission to find and kill a basilisk that had been devouring fishermen and livestock from a nearby village for months.

The beast was elusive. It hunted at night, moved through the rivers and wells of the area, and disappeared before dawn. The squad had tried tracking it for weeks without success. It was always one step ahead.

So they changed strategy. If they couldn't find it, they would make it find them.

They evacuated the village. Placed livestock as bait in the central plaza. And waited.

Jihyo, Chaeyoung, and another guy, whose name Jihyo really couldn't remember, watched from the roof of a house, hidden in the shadows. The full moon illuminated the plaza with silver light.

For hours, nothing. Just the wind and the occasional nervous bleating of the sheep tied in the center.

Then, she felt it. A vibration on the roof. Subtle, rhythmic. Something was slithering.

"Here it comes," Chaeyoung whispered, her hand closing around her sword handle.

It emerged from the shadows of an alley like a nightmare made flesh.

The basilisk was larger than the reports described. Its body snaked through the plaza, endless, covered in black scales that gleamed wet under the moonlight. Along its back, crests of spines bristled like the spines of an abyssal fish. Its body undulated with horrible grace, sliding silently despite its size, leaving a wet trail on the stone.

But the worst were the eyes. Yellow, with vertical pupils that scanned the plaza with predatory intelligence. And beneath them, a mouth that slowly opened, revealing rows of curved fangs. A thick, dark liquid dripped from them, falling to the ground with a hiss. Where it fell, the stone smoked.

_Venom_, Jihyo thought, feeling a shiver.

The creature advanced toward the sheep, its forked tongue tasting the air.

The plan was to wait for it to start eating. Ambush it while it was distracted. Aim for the weak points: the eyes, the soft part under the jaw.

But plans were never perfect.

Movement in the stables. Jihyo turned her head and felt her stomach drop.

A small figure was coming out of the stable, pulling the rope of a colt that refused to move.

_Shit._

"There's a child," she warned in a barely audible whisper.

"I see him," Chaeyoung responded, tense.

The boy was pulling the colt desperately. The animal whinnied low, terrified, digging its hooves into the ground. The boy stumbled, falling against some stacked tools. The metallic crash echoed through the plaza like a bell.

The basilisk turned its head. Its yellow eyes fixed on the boy.

For a moment, everything went silent. Then, the creature hissed. A low, vibrating sound that Jihyo felt in her bones. And it began sliding toward the stable.

"We have to do something!" Jihyo stood up, but Chaeyoung stopped her with a hand on her shoulder.

"That thing will kill us if we face it like this! Better him than us!" said the other boy who came with them, backing away.

Jihyo looked at him with disgust, clenching her fist. She turned toward him, ready to hit him.

"Captain." Chaeyoung's voice stopped her. It was calm, firm. "I'll distract it. Get the kid out."

It wasn't an order. It was a plan. The only one they had now.

Jihyo nodded and jumped from the roof, rolling upon landing.

"HEY!" Chaeyoung's shout tore through the silence. The sound of her sword striking her armor echoed through the plaza. "OVER HERE, BEAST!"

The basilisk stopped. Its head turned toward the sound with an unnatural movement, too fast for something that size.

Chaeyoung was standing on the edge of the roof, her silhouette outlined against the moon.

The creature hissed and changed direction, sliding toward her with terrifying speed. Its body knocked down a wooden post as if it were a branch, its mass destroying everything in its path.

Jihyo didn't look back. She ran toward the stable.

The boy was on the ground, his foot trapped under a fallen beam, the colt's rope tangled around his wrist. The animal was pulling, terrified, making everything worse.

"My horse!" the boy shouted when he saw her.

"Forget the horse." Jihyo cut the rope with one slash. The colt bolted into the night. Then she drove her sword into the ground, using it as a lever under the beam.

"No! Come back!" the boy shouted, watching his animal flee.

"Shut up and push!" Jihyo grunted, her muscles burning as she lifted the wood.

The boy, to his credit, obeyed. He pushed backward with his free leg until he could pull his foot out. Jihyo grabbed him and threw him over her shoulder.

"Hey! Be care—!"

"One more word and I'll leave you here!"

In the distance, the sound of destruction. Wood breaking. A roar that wasn't human.

Jihyo ran.

She left the boy behind a stone wall, away from the plaza. "Don't move."

"As if I could," the boy said, pointing to his swollen ankle.

_This damn kid._

Jihyo ran back to the plaza and stopped dead.

The roof where they had been no longer existed. The house was rubble. And in the middle of the destruction, Chaeyoung was running between buildings, the basilisk chasing her like a deadly shadow, its body knocking down everything in its path.

Chaeyoung was fast, but the creature was faster. Every time she turned a corner, the basilisk closed the distance. Its tail whipped a wall, sending stones flying.

"Chaeyoung!" Jihyo shouted.

"The blacksmith's roof!" Chaeyoung's voice came broken as she ran. "I need height!"

Jihyo understood. If Chaeyoung could jump onto the creature from above...

But the basilisk was between them and the blacksmith.

"Hey, oversized worm!" Jihyo struck her sword against an abandoned shield on the ground. The clang echoed through the plaza.

The basilisk stopped. Its head turned toward Jihyo. Those yellow eyes found her.

_Shit._

The creature hissed and changed targets. It launched itself at Jihyo with impossible speed, its jaws open, venom dripping.

Jihyo ran toward a narrow alley. Too narrow for the basilisk's body. The head entered, jaws snapping inches from her back, but the body got stuck between the walls.

The creature roared in frustration, thrashing, its body hitting the walls as it tried to advance.

Jihyo exited the other side of the alley, panting. She had bought Chaeyoung time. But not much.

The basilisk retreated, its scales scraping against the walls, and circled the building. It was learning. Adapting.

Then she saw it.

Chaeyoung was on the blacksmith's roof. Three stories high. Sword in hand, waiting.

The basilisk saw her too. It stopped in the plaza, watching her. For a moment, neither moved.

Then the creature launched itself at the building. Not at the door. At the wall.

The impact shook the entire structure. Bricks fell. The roof swayed.

"Chaeyoung, jump!" Jihyo shouted.

Chaeyoung jumped. Not downward. Toward the basilisk.

She landed on its back with a dull thud. Her hands searched for grip among the scales, but the spines along its spine cut her palms. She screamed in pain but didn't let go.

The basilisk went berserk. It thrashed violently, its body slamming against buildings, trying to shake off the intruder. Chaeyoung clung with legs and one hand, the other holding her sword.

She looked for a gap between the scales. A weak point. She drove her sword down with all her strength.

The blade bounced off.

The scales were too thick. Like trying to stab stone.

The basilisk spun, slamming its body against a wall. Chaeyoung felt the impact in every bone. Her grip loosened.

_No. I'm not dying like this!_

She crawled forward over the beast's back, the spines tearing her clothes, cutting her skin. Warm blood ran down her arms. But she kept moving.

Toward the head.

The basilisk felt her movement and shook its head violently. Chaeyoung went flying, but managed to drive her sword into the creature's neck at the last second. She hung there, her feet swinging over the void, held only by the weapon buried between two scales.

The beast roared and shook its neck. The sword was coming loose.

Chaeyoung looked up. The basilisk's head was right there. And one of its yellow eyes was looking directly at her, full of hatred.

She didn't think. She just acted.

She pushed herself upward with all her strength, ripping the sword out in the movement, and drove the blade directly into the creature's eye.

The roar that followed was deafening.

The basilisk shook its head with demented violence, blinded by pain and fury. Chaeyoung lost her grip and went flying, hitting the ground hard. Her vision darkened for a moment.

When she could focus again, she saw the creature staggering, black blood gushing from its destroyed eye. It was wounded. But not dead.

And it was furious.

The basilisk found her with its remaining eye. It opened its jaws, venom dripping, and prepared to charge.

Chaeyoung tried to get up. Her arms weren't responding well. Everything hurt.

_Move. Move!_

Then, a figure appeared between her and the beast.

Jihyo.

"Don't you dare die!" Jihyo shouted, her sword raised, standing between Chaeyoung and the creature.

The basilisk charged.

Jihyo dodged by inches, rolling to the side, dragging Chaeyoung with her. The creature's head hit the ground where she had been, cracking the stone. But Jihyo was already on her feet, running toward an overturned cart.

"The fishing net!" she shouted, pointing.

Chaeyoung saw what Jihyo had seen. A thick net, the kind used to catch large prey in the river, hung from a post next to the cart.

The basilisk recovered and turned toward Jihyo, hissing.

Chaeyoung forced herself to stand. Every muscle screamed in protest. She ran toward the post, limping, her sword dragging.

Jihyo kept attracting the beast's attention, dodging charges, keeping it occupied. But she was tiring. Each dodge was slower than the last.

Chaeyoung reached the post. The net was held by a thick rope. She cut it with one slash.

"Jihyo, bring it here!"

Jihyo didn't respond with words. She just ran toward Chaeyoung, the basilisk on her heels.

At the last moment, Jihyo threw herself to the ground, rolling.

Chaeyoung released the net.

It fell over the basilisk's head, tangling in its horns, its spines, its jaws. The creature thrashed, trying to free itself, but each movement tangled it more.

"The post!" Chaeyoung shouted.

Both ran toward the enormous wooden beam that held up the cart structure. They started pushing, their boots slipping on the ground, their muscles burning.

The wood creaked. It tilted.

The basilisk was freeing itself from the net. Its body twisted, tearing the ropes.

"Harder!" Jihyo roared.

With one last effort, the post gave way. It fell on the basilisk's head with a solid crunch, pinning it to the ground.

The creature writhed, but it was trapped. Immobilized. Its remaining eye searched frantically, its tail whipping the air.

Chaeyoung didn't wait.

She climbed onto the post, ran along it, and when she reached the beast's head, she raised her sword with both hands.

The basilisk looked at her with its one eye. There was something almost human in that gaze. Fear.

Chaeyoung drove her sword into its skull.

The blade sank to the hilt. The basilisk's body convulsed once, twice, and then went still.

Chaeyoung didn't let go of the sword. She stayed there, panting, trembling, until she was sure it was dead.

When she finally climbed down from the creature, she could barely stand. Her hands bled from the cuts from the spines. Her body was a map of bruises and wounds. But she was alive.

Jihyo approached her, equally wrecked, breathing hard.

For a moment, they just looked at each other.

Chaeyoung might be smaller than her, in height, weight, or age, but she was undoubtedly tough. Brave. Strong in her own way. And she had just saved her life.

"What happened to the kid?" Chaeyoung asked between breaths.

"Oh, I left him by one of the walls over there." Jihyo pointed in the direction with her hand. "He's hurt his foot," she finished saying, before hurried footsteps approached them, putting them on alert for a few seconds before seeing the other knight who had come with them. "You! Where the hell did you go?" she shouted furiously. Before the guy could say anything, Jihyo punched him in the cheek, making him fall, groaning in pain. "A child almost died and you run from the fight?!" she yelled, grabbing him by the armor, lifting him slightly, and preparing another blow when she felt a hand on her shoulder.

"Captain, wait," Chaeyoung's calm voice stopped her. Jihyo turned to look at her, still furious, but let go of the boy reluctantly.

Chaeyoung approached the guy, who was still on the ground holding his cheek. "Are you okay?" she asked in an almost kind tone, offering him her hand to help him up.

The guy looked at her with relief, taking her hand. "Y-yes, it's just that I only—"

"Let me see." Chaeyoung helped him to his feet, examining his face with apparent concern. "She hit you hard."

"Yeah, I didn't want to, but the situation—" the guy let his guard down.

As soon as he removed his hand from his cheek, another punch, now from Chaeyoung, knocked him down again. The guy fell to the ground, clearly disoriented.

"What the hell?" was all he could yell while groaning in pain.

Jihyo let out a short laugh, surprised. _Didn't expect that._

Chaeyoung quickly grabbed him by the armor, placing her sword at the guy's throat, who looked at her scared.

"No one. I repeat, no one abandons innocents to save their own skin." The sword pressed harder against his throat. "Next time, I hope you die in combat, or else, I'll personally feed you to one of these things, understood?" The guy just mumbled fearfully, saying incoherent things. In a surprisingly quick movement, Chaeyoung stood him up and slammed him against the lifeless head of the serpent, near its fangs, her sword now at the back of his neck, preventing the guy from pulling away.

"Yes, yes! Sorry! I'm sorry, Chaeyoung!" the guy screamed.

"Lieutenant." Jihyo's firm voice resonated behind them.

The guy turned to look at her, confused and scared. Jihyo watched him with crossed arms, her expression serious.

"When you address her, it's Lieutenant Chaeyoung," she clarified with authority. "Understood?"

The boy nodded frantically, turning his gaze back to the sword at his neck. "Y-yes! Sorry, Lieutenant! I'm sorry, Lieutenant Chaeyoung!"

Chaeyoung's eyes met Jihyo's for a brief moment. The captain gave her a slight nod. _I trust you._

Chaeyoung, without saying anything more, satisfied with the response, released the boy. He fell to the ground on his own, trying to get away from the basilisk and crawling backward in fear.

"Go get the horses," the younger one mentioned calmly, sheathing her sword as if nothing had happened while looking at the guy still on the ground.

He just stood up quickly and ran toward where their camp was located.

Jihyo approached Chaeyoung, a half smile on her face. "Lieutenant," she repeated in a lighter tone, almost teasing.

"Captain," Chaeyoung responded with the same tone, a small smile appearing.

Something had changed between them. Jihyo could feel it. She no longer saw a girl who had been assigned to her by force. She saw someone loyal to her principles. Someone who had saved her life. Who thought like her and was willing to sacrifice herself for the necessary cause. Someone she could trust.

_Maybe it wasn't so bad that they assigned her to me_, Jihyo thought.

"Jihyo," the younger one approached her, looking at her firmly. "Lead the way." To which Jihyo quickly snapped out of her trance and started walking toward where she had left the insolent child.

The soft clink of their armor resonated as they walked. "Are you hurt?" Chaeyoung asked.

"I'm fine. Just some scratches, nothing serious," she responded. After a bit of silence, she asked: "And you?"

"Same," she responded simply. "And the kid, is it serious?"

"I don't think so. It was just the blow," Jihyo responded. "He was complaining without any problem, so I think he'll be fine," she finished saying, laughing a little as she remembered the kid's attitude. "The kid was trying to save his colt. He ran off in this same direction as soon as I freed him."

Chaeyoung just listened attentively, walking beside her.

"About time!" another complaint from the kid was heard as soon as he spotted the knights in the distance.

"What did I tell you?" Jihyo whispered with the same annoyance as the kid. Chaeyoung just let out a small laugh.

"The wolves haven't eaten you? Damn," Jihyo joked before kneeling beside him. Chaeyoung did the same, carefully bringing her hands closer to examine the boy, feeling his leg while he continued arguing with the older one.

"Indeed, it's just a bruise. You're going to get a pretty ugly bruise and it's going to hurt quite a bit for a few days, but you should be able to walk again without a problem afterward," Chaeyoung commented, standing up.

"Are you sure? It hurts a lot," the boy answered with a face of discomfort.

"Oh, we could just cut off your leg! No leg, no pain!" Jihyo joked, carefully picking up the boy.

Shortly after, Jihyo took him over her shoulder again while he complained, and they walked back to the village, where the sun was already rising again. A brilliant orange covered the sky.

---

"Captain, the forest is clear," a scout announced aloud, pulling Jihyo from her memories.

"And the wounded?" she asked quickly.

"They're already being transported for treatment. Casualties were minimal," the scout commented again.

"Excellent. Our work here is done then." She looked at Chaeyoung, giving her a pat on the shoulder of her armor. "Let's go home, first round's on me." A cheerful roar was heard across the field.

Chaeyoung walked across the field, stepping on broken shields, swords, mud, and fallen trolls. "Cricket, come here boy," she called to her horse softly. The black thoroughbred neighed happily, bringing his head close to the knight. Chaeyoung mounted in a quick and experienced movement, galloping down the path heading home.

---

After a few hours of travel, Chaeyoung arrived at Twiceland Castle. She dismounted and headed calmly to the stables, gently pulling the reins.

A slight pain in her side made itself present as she walked. It wasn't unusual for Chaeyoung to get hurt, though these days it was a bit less frequent. _I should go get this checked out._

"Chae!" Jeongyeon's cheerful voice made her look up. The older woman approached her animatedly, opening her arms to hug her. Chaeyoung received her gladly, suppressing the groan in her side so as not to draw the older woman's attention. "How did it go? Did you kill many trolls?" she said excitedly, making gestures with her hands, wielding an imaginary sword that she waved from side to side, "attacking" Chaeyoung.

"It went very well. The trolls in the surrounding areas shouldn't be a problem anymore," she answered between laughs, offering her Cricket's reins.

"And how is this boy doing?" she said, taking the reins and stroking the horse's head, who neighed happily to see the woman.

"I swear, he's happier to see you than when he sees me," she said with some envy, though amused at the situation while discreetly holding her side in a comfortable position.

"It's because you don't know where to scratch him where he likes it," she said while scratching behind his ear. The horse neighed and stamped contentedly with a front hoof.

"You're such a sellout," she said offended to the horse. Jeongyeon continued petting the thoroughbred animatedly, while in the distance, Chaeyoung could see Princess Nayeon watching the scene with a happy smile. "And it seems he's not the only one who gets happy to see you," Chaeyoung mentioned quietly while giving a slight bow in the distance. The pain in her side pinching at the action. _Damn._

At the comment, Jeongyeon turned to look at Chaeyoung and then toward where she was looking, smiling back when she saw the princess, who upon meeting eyes, waved at her animatedly and started walking toward them.

"What can I say, I have that effect on girls," Jeongyeon raised an eyebrow at Chaeyoung's disgusted look.

"Please, never say that again," the younger one pleaded dryly.

"Jeongyeon, Knight Chaeyoung," the princess greeted upon arriving. Both gave a bow before her. Chaeyoung felt the pain increase with each breath.

"Your Highness, it's a pleasure to see you," she greeted the future queen kindly.

"Indeed, it's been a while. How are the expeditions going, dear Chaeyoung?" she asked gracefully.

"We've controlled the situation in the north. The trolls have retreated to their territory. We're also taking control of the situation with the bandits in the south through various checkpoints for trade, Your Highness." Chaeyoung's response came automatically, accustomed to giving reports to the royal family and her superiors.

"Those are wonderful news. I must say I've heard a lot about you. Captain Jihyo speaks very highly of her second-in-command," the princess looked at her with interest. "They say you're the best sword in the kingdom."

"Captain Jihyo is very generous with her words, Your Highness," Chaeyoung answered, bowing her head.

"Would you be interested in a somewhat more... interesting job?" The question caught the knight off guard.

Chaeyoung hesitated a bit on how to approach the question. Jeongyeon watched her with attention and curiosity, still holding Cricket by the reins.

"Anything I can help Your Highness with, I'd be delighted," she finally answered.

"I'm looking for a personal guard for Princess Mina. As you may know, the last one turned out to be... rather inefficient." _Incompetent, she means._ "It would be a complement to your current duties, of course. I don't intend to steal Captain Jihyo's best element," a small smile appeared on the princess's face. "Would you be interested in such a noble task?"

_It's not like I can refuse the princess, future queen, anyway._ Though being honest, the idea wasn't displeasing to her. "It would be an honor for me. If Your Highness asks it of me, I will comply," she finally responded. Chaeyoung gave another bow, her side giving no more, but maintaining composure.

"I'll speak with the King then. Expect news from me soon," she said before turning her gaze to Jeongyeon, smiling sweetly and taking her arm as they walked to the stables with Cricket.

_Royal guard for Princess Mina._ Chaeyoung walked through the long corridors of the castle, her boots echoing as she walked, accompanied by the clink of armor. _I wonder how Jihyo will take it._

She stopped in front of a brown door at the end of the hallway. She knocked on the door with three strong knocks before entering.

"I figured it was you," Tzuyu's voice greeted her. "I heard you finally returned from the expedition," she said, getting up from her chair, indicating with a hand for her to sit on the bed. From experience, she already knew why Chaeyoung had come.

Chaeyoung sat down and began removing her chest plate. A light but thick metal that had saved her life countless times. Tzuyu brought a chair to the edge of the bed to sit in front of Chaeyoung and, seeing her sister's difficulty, approached to help her.

Chaeyoung felt fortunate for many things in her life, and that her sister worked in the castle as a royal healer was, by far, one of those things.

"Okay, take a deep breath," Tzuyu instructed, feeling her ribs. Chaeyoung complied, breathing deeply with some difficulty and a groan mid-breath.

Tzuyu leaned closer, observing the area closely. She pressed with her fingers at different points, noticing the swelling.

"Ouch," Chaeyoung complained.

"From 1 to 10?" her sister asked.

"6, maybe 7. Every time I breathe deeply, it hurts." Tzuyu nodded, getting up toward a shelf full of jars and herbs.

"It's not broken, but you have a severe contusion. The muscles around your ribs are very inflamed," she explained while selecting some ingredients. "A troll, I suppose."

"Threw me like a rag doll," Chaeyoung admitted with a grimace.

Tzuyu returned with a mortar, mixing herbs with a strong-smelling ointment. "This is going to hurt a little, but it will reduce the swelling," she warned before beginning to apply the mixture to Chaeyoung's side with firm but careful movements.

Chaeyoung gritted her teeth, hissing at the burning. "A little?"

"Don't be a whiner," Tzuyu responded unfazed, continuing with her work. "I'm going to bandage you so you don't move too much. You need to rest at least a week. No combat, no intense training."

"A week?" Chaeyoung protested.

"Minimum." Tzuyu looked at her seriously while taking some linen bandages. "If you don't rest, this can become something worse. And next time I won't be so gentle."

Chaeyoung huffed but let her sister carefully bandage her around the torso.

"Well, it seems destiny wanted it this way," she said resignedly while Tzuyu finished adjusting the bandages. "Princess Nayeon offered me to be Princess Mina's royal guard."

Tzuyu raised an eyebrow, interested. "Really?" She finished tying the bandage and wiped her hands on a cloth. "I've heard Princess Mina is very calm. Maybe a little less adventure is just what you need," she said while putting away the ointment and leftover herbs.

"A congratulations would have been nice there."

"Congratulations, little one," Tzuyu said with a smile, handing her a small jar. "Apply this every night before bed. And if the pain gets worse, come immediately, understood?"

Chaeyoung took the jar, nodding. "Thanks, Tzu."

"That's what sisters are for," she responded, giving her a gentle pat on her healthy shoulder before returning to her desk.

Shortly after, Chaeyoung took her chest plate and headed toward her room in the east wing of the castle, where most of the guards and other staff members also lived.

She opened her door wearily, tossed her chest plate on a nearby table, and removed her sword belt, leaning it against the bed. She took off the rest of her armor and then her boots. She freshened up as quickly as possible, hoping to rest a bit before dinner or before her presence was required.

She lay down carefully on her soft bed. It wasn't big, but thanks to Chaeyoung's small size, she fit without much problem. She relaxed her muscles, feeling the exhaustion overcome her, and fell deeply asleep.

---

A knock on the door woke her.

Chaeyoung opened her eyes slowly, disoriented. The orange light of sunset entered through the arched window of her room, tinting the gray stone walls with warm tones. The fire in the small fireplace had reduced to embers.

Another knock.

"Chaeyoung! Dinner!" Momo's voice sounded muffled on the other side.

"Coming," she responded, sitting up carefully. The pain in her side was still there, but Tzuyu's bandages and the rest had helped. It was no longer a stabbing pain, but a dull ache she could ignore.

She got up and carefully stretched her arms, avoiding straining her side. Her room was small but functional: a wooden bed with gray sheets, a trunk at the foot where she kept her belongings, a modest desk with some papers and a wooden figure of a tiger that Tzuyu had given her years ago. The kingdom's banner hung by the window, a white hummingbird embroidered on a golden background, and a woven rug covered part of the stone floor.

She approached the basin in the corner and washed her face with cold water. Then she opened the trunk and took out her castle clothes: a dark green shirt with subtle embroidery on the collar and cuffs, fastened with laces at the front, and brown pants held by a leather belt. She dressed carefully, adjusting the shirt over the bandages.

She hesitated for a moment before taking her sword. Technically, she didn't need it inside the castle, but she felt strange without it. She hooked it to her belt and left the room.

The knights' dining hall buzzed with activity.

It was a spacious hall with a ceiling of dark wooden beams that arched over the tables. Iron chandeliers hung from the ceiling, their candles illuminating the place with a warm light that mixed with the glow of torches on the walls. Large windows let in the last rays of sunset, and the aroma of freshly baked bread, roasted meat, and stew filled the air.

Long tables of solid wood occupied the center, surrounded by knights who conversed, laughed, and ate after a long day. The noise of cutlery, clinking mugs, and overlapping voices created a constant and comforting murmur.

Chaeyoung spotted Jihyo at one of the tables near the window. The captain had already freshened up: her dark hair fell loose over her shoulders, and she wore a shirt similar to Chaeyoung's but in burgundy, her sword resting on her belt as always. Even off duty, Jihyo maintained that natural air of authority.

Beside her, Momo was devouring a plate of stew with impressive energy. Her hair was pulled back in a messy ponytail and bread crumbs were at the corner of her lips.

"Chaefoung!" Momo exclaimed with her mouth full, waving a hand.

"Chew first," Jihyo looked at her with a mixture of disgust and resignation.

Chaeyoung sat across from them, a smile appearing on her face. "How many plates have you had?"

Momo held up three fingers, still chewing.

"We've been here ten minutes," Jihyo added.

"It's a talent," Chaeyoung served herself some stew from the pot in the center of the table.

"Ith a..." Momo swallowed with effort, hitting her chest. "It's a gift. You have to make the most of it."

"How's the side?" Jihyo asked, lowering her voice.

"Better. Tzuyu bandaged me well." Chaeyoung tried the stew. It was delicious. "A week of rest, she says."

"A week without trolls throwing you. What a tragedy," Jihyo smiled sideways.

"I'm going to miss flying through the air, honestly."

Jeongyeon appeared then, sliding onto the bench next to Chaeyoung with a plate in hand. "What did I miss?"

"Momo trying to talk," Jihyo responded.

"Ah, the usual then."

Momo ignored them completely, focused on tearing off a piece of bread to dip it in the sauce.

"How's Cricket?" Chaeyoung asked.

"Good. I brushed him and fed him. He was tired but happy," Jeongyeon bit into a piece of meat. "That horse adores you."

"It's mutual."

"By the way," Jeongyeon lowered her voice, leaning toward Chaeyoung, "did you get an answer about Princess Mina yet?"

Chaeyoung shook her head. "Not yet. Princess Nayeon said she would talk to the King, but I haven't heard anything."

"What about Princess Mina?" Jihyo raised an eyebrow, interested.

Before Chaeyoung could respond, a cheerful voice interrupted.

"Good evening, warriors!"

Dahyun appeared with a radiant smile, her leather apron still stained with soot. She dropped onto the bench next to Momo, who barely looked up from her plate to greet her with a grunt.

"Just get out of the forge?" Jeongyeon asked.

"I finished a beautiful sword. Beautiful." Dahyun clasped her hands with excitement. "The balance is perfect, the edge could cut a hair in the air, and the engraving on the guard... a work of art, if I may say so."

"You're the only one who says so," Jihyo murmured, though she was smiling.

"Someone has to appreciate my genius." Dahyun served herself food without losing her enthusiasm. "And what are we celebrating? I heard you came back victorious."

"The trolls won't be bothering anyone for a good while," Jihyo confirmed.

"Excellent! I'll drink to that." Dahyun raised her mug. "Momo?"

Momo raised her mug without stopping chewing, her eyes fixed on a particularly juicy piece of meat.

"Your enthusiasm moves me," Dahyun said dryly.

The conversation flowed naturally between jokes and comments about the battle, the new weapons Dahyun was forging, and the horses Jeongyeon had been training. Chaeyoung allowed herself to relax, enjoying the moment. These were the moments that made everything else worth it.

It was then that Jihyo looked up toward the entrance. Her expression changed instantly.

"Her Highness in the hall!" she exclaimed firmly, standing up.

The effect was immediate. Conversations cut off, mugs stopped clinking, and the knights stood up almost in unison, standing at attention with respect.

Princess Nayeon was at the door.

She wore an elegant dress in blue tones, her hair gathered delicately. Two guards flanked her, but she dismissed them with a gesture while her eyes scanned the hall.

Momo, who had just stuffed an enormous piece of bread in her mouth, choked trying to stand at attention quickly. Dahyun patted her back while containing her laughter.

"Please, continue," Nayeon said with a kind smile, gesturing for everyone to sit back down. Her eyes found Jeongyeon for a moment, her smile becoming softer, before addressing Chaeyoung.

"Knight Chaeyoung," she approached their table. "I'm sorry to interrupt your dinner, but I have good news."

Chaeyoung felt her companions' gazes on her. "Your Highness?"

"The King has approved my request. Officially, you are the new personal guard of Princess Mina." Nayeon smiled. "If you'll accompany me, I'd like to introduce you to her tonight, but you will start duties tomorrow."

Chaeyoung exchanged a quick glance with Jihyo, who nodded slightly.

"It would be an honor, Your Highness."

"Excellent." Nayeon glanced briefly at Jeongyeon, before turning toward the door. "Follow me, Chaeyoung."

Chaeyoung said goodbye to her companions with a gesture. Momo gave a thumbs up, still coughing a little. Dahyun winked at her. Jihyo simply nodded, but there was something like pride in her gaze.

The castle corridors looked different at night.

Chaeyoung was used to the east wing, where the knights and service staff lived. Functional corridors of gray stone, simple torches, unadorned wooden doors.

But as she followed Princess Nayeon toward the royal wing, everything changed.

The ceilings rose in elaborate arches, decorated with moldings and frescoes she could barely make out in the dimness. Marble statues flanked the corridor at regular intervals, figures of ancient kings and queens watching with empty eyes. The floor was no longer rough stone but polished tiles forming geometric patterns, and the torches were replaced by wrought iron candelabras that cast dancing shadows on the walls.

Her boots echoed differently here, sharper, lonelier.

"Princess Mina is usually in her chambers or on the garden terrace at this hour," Nayeon commented as they walked. "She's a quiet young woman. She prefers reading and music to social events."

"I understand, Your Highness."

"I know you're used to action, Chaeyoung. Battles, monsters, blood." Nayeon looked at her sideways. "Protecting Mina will be... different."

"Different doesn't mean less important."

Nayeon smiled. "That's why I chose you."

They exited to an exterior corridor that bordered one of the castle's inner gardens. Stone arches opened onto a courtyard covered in ivy, where statues rose among carefully trimmed bushes. The air was fresh and smelled of damp earth and night flowers. The moon, almost full, bathed everything in silver light.

They continued along a cobblestone path that crossed another garden, this one smaller and more intimate. Vines with reddish and golden leaves climbed up a wooden pergola, creating a natural roof of autumn colors. Fallen petals decorated the ground like a delicate carpet.

Nayeon stopped before reaching the terrace.

"Chaeyoung." Her tone had changed. She was no longer the friendly princess who joked with Jeongyeon in the stables. There was seriousness in her voice, genuine concern.

Chaeyoung stopped beside her, waiting.

"As you may know, this job may seem calm," Nayeon looked ahead, where the terrace could be glimpsed among the bushes. "But it's dangerous. More than it appears."

"Your Highness?"

Nayeon was silent for a moment, as if choosing her words carefully.

"My sister has received threats. Several, in recent months." Her eyes darkened. "Not simple letters from fanatics or disgruntled nobles. Real threats. Coordinated."

Chaeyoung frowned. "Why would anyone want to harm Princess Mina?"

Nayeon let out a sigh.

"There's a... prophecy." The word left her lips with some discomfort. "An old tale, rather. It says that the youngest daughter of three sisters, in the twentieth generation of the royal family, will be of strange origin but possessor of immense power." She paused. "Mina is the youngest of three. And this is the twentieth generation."

Chaeyoung processed the information in silence.

"I'm not a fan of rumors," Nayeon continued, her voice firmer. "And I'm certainly not superstitious. I don't believe in prophecies or old wives' tales." She looked her directly in the eyes. "But there are those who do believe. Some see her as a blessing. Others..." she left the sentence hanging.

"As a threat," Chaeyoung completed.

"Exactly." Nayeon nodded. "I don't care if the prophecy is real or not. What I care about is that my sister is in danger. And I need someone to protect her. Someone I can trust."

"Is that why you chose me?"

"I chose you because you're the best. Because you don't run from danger. And because I know you'll protect Mina with your life if necessary." Nayeon held her gaze. "Am I wrong?"

"No, Your Highness."

"Good." Nayeon relaxed her shoulders a little. "Take care of her, Chaeyoung. Protect her. And you will be rewarded accordingly. You have my word."

"I don't need rewards, Your Highness. I just need to know what I'm up against."

Nayeon smiled, though there was sadness in her eyes.

"If I knew for certain, I would tell you. For now, I only know that someone wants to harm my sister." She resumed walking toward the terrace. "And I'm not going to allow it."

Chaeyoung followed her, her mind processing everything she had just heard. A prophecy. Threats. A young princess, oblivious to everything moving in the shadows around her.

_This job is going to be anything but calm_, she thought.

Finally, they arrived at an elevated terrace in one of the minor towers.

The place was beautiful. Stone columns framed an open space overlooking the distant mountains and the kingdom's fields. The sky stretched vast and starry, and the breeze was soft and fresh.

And there, sitting at a small table by the railing, was Princess Mina.

The candlelight on the table softly illuminated her face. She had an open book in her hands and a steaming cup of tea beside her. Her dark hair fell over her shoulders, and she wore a simple but elegant dress in lavender tones. There was something ethereal about her, as if she belonged to a painting rather than the real world.

When she heard the footsteps, Mina looked up from the book. Her eyes found Nayeon first, and then Chaeyoung. She stood up gracefully, leaving the book on the table.

"Sister," she greeted Nayeon with a subtle bow.

"Mina." Nayeon approached and briefly took her hands with affection. "I present to you Knight Chaeyoung, your new personal guard."

Mina directed her gaze to Chaeyoung. Her eyes were dark and serene, difficult to read.

Chaeyoung gave a formal bow. "It's an honor to meet you, Your Highness. I promise to protect you with my life."

"The honor is mine," Mina responded. Her voice was soft, melodious, perfectly modulated. "I've heard much about your deeds, Knight Chaeyoung. The best sword in the kingdom, they say."

"People exaggerate, Your Highness."

"Humility is an admirable virtue." Mina gestured toward the table. "Would you like to sit? I can ask for more tea."

"I'm afraid I must retire," Nayeon said. "But I'll leave you to get acquainted. Chaeyoung, I'll see you tomorrow to discuss the details of your new duties."

"Yes, Your Highness."

Nayeon gave Mina one last squeeze of her hands before withdrawing, her footsteps fading down the stone path.

A brief silence settled between them.

Mina sat back down, indicating with an elegant gesture the chair across from her. Chaeyoung hesitated for a moment before accepting, sitting with her back straight, not fully leaning back.

"You don't have to be so tense, Knight," Mina said, a shadow of a smile on her lips. "I don't bite."

"Forgive me, Your Highness. It's not my intention—"

"It's alright." Mina took a porcelain teapot that rested on the table and, with graceful movements, poured tea into a second cup. The golden liquid fell in a perfect stream, without a single drop out of place. She offered the cup to Chaeyoung. "Here. It's freshly made."

"Thank you, Your Highness." Chaeyoung accepted the cup carefully, feeling the warmth seep through the porcelain.

Mina picked up her book again, opening it where she had left off. Her eyes glided over the pages softly, completely absorbed.

Chaeyoung took a sip of tea. It was smooth, with a floral note she didn't recognize. She allowed herself to observe the princess as she read: the way her fingers turned the pages delicately, how her lips moved almost imperceptibly with each verse, the absolute peace that emanated from her.

It was a comfortable silence, strangely.

After a few moments, Mina looked up, as if sensing the gaze.

"I imagine this is very different from what you're used to," she said. "Battles and monsters are more exciting than taking care of a princess who only reads and drinks tea."

Chaeyoung looked at her, surprised by the frankness.

"Every duty has its importance, Your Highness," she responded honestly. "And protecting someone is protecting someone, regardless of whether it's on a battlefield or on a terrace."

Mina observed her for a moment, as if evaluating her. Then she nodded slightly.

"I think we'll get along well, Knight Chaeyoung."

"I hope so, Your Highness."

Mina returned her attention to the book, though she didn't open it. Her fingers absently caressed the cover.

"Do you like poetry?" she asked suddenly.

"I haven't had much time to read, to be honest."

"Perhaps I can change that." Mina looked up, and for the first time, Chaeyoung noticed a glimmer of something beyond the perfect composure. Curiosity? Shyness? It was hard to tell. "I have many books. Too many, according to my sister."

"I don't think there's such a thing as too many books, Your Highness."

Mina smiled. A small smile, almost imperceptible, but genuine.

"We'll definitely get along well."

The night breeze swayed the leaves of the nearby vine, and Chaeyoung allowed herself to relax her shoulders, just a little.

This new chapter of her life promised to be very different from everything she knew.

But perhaps, she thought as she watched the princess return to her book under the candlelight, that wasn't a bad thing.
